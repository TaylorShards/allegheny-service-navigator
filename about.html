Our Mission

The core mission of the Allegheny Service Navigator is to restore dignity and empower individuals in crisis by bridging the systemic information gap in social service delivery. We believe that access to clear, accurate, and actionable information is a fundamental right that enables pathways to stability.

For too long, the burden of navigating a complex web of outdated resource lists and unspoken eligibility requirements has fallen on those least equipped to handle it. This systemic friction leads to wasted time, profound frustration, and missed opportunities for critical assistance. Our objective is to eliminate that friction by creating a single, reliable source of truth—a living atlas of services that reflects the reality on the ground, not just on paper.

Our Data Strategy

A directory is only as good as the reliability and structure of its data. Our strategy is built on three foundational pillars to ensure the ASN is a trusted, authoritative resource for case managers, county administrators, and clients alike.

1. Lived Experience as a Primary Source

Our data collection model is uniquely focused on capturing "ground-truth" data. We actively incorporate feedback from individuals with lived experience in transitional programs and the frontline case managers who support them. This allows us to document not just what services are officially available, but their real-world operational status, their true eligibility hurdles, and the actual resolution pathways that work.

2. Structured & Semantic Data

We are moving beyond simple text lists. By leveraging a powerful MediaWiki backend with the Semantic MediaWiki extension, we structure every data point. Service hours, capacity, eligibility criteria, and location are not just text—they are queryable fields. This enables complex, filtered searches (e.g., "Show me all shelters with available beds that accept pets within 2 miles") that are impossible with traditional directories.

3. Verification and Integrity Workflow

Trust is paramount. The platform incorporates a mandatory verification workflow (via the FlaggedRevisions extension) for all data entries. Information submitted by the community or updated by a service provider is held in a pending state until a trained administrator or a designated partner organization has officially verified its accuracy. This ensures that every piece of information on the Navigator has been vetted, providing a layer of confidence for all users.

Our Partners

The Allegheny Service Navigator is a collaborative effort that stands on the shoulders of both community leaders and pioneering technology providers. We are actively seeking to build and strengthen these relationships.

Community & Institutional Partners

We are actively engaging with and seeking formal collaboration with key regional stakeholders, including the Allegheny County Department of Human Services (DHS), the Office of Vocational Rehabilitation (OVR), the Office of Community Health and Safety (CHS), and numerous local non-profit organizations and NGOs who are on the front lines of service delivery.

Technology & Infrastructure Partners

Our work is made possible by the generous support provided by forward-thinking technology companies.

  • GitHub: We leverage the GitHub platform for transparent, secure, and collaborative development of our codebase. As a non-profit entity, we are applying for the GitHub for Non-profits program to access advanced tools and support for our mission.
  • The Wikimedia Foundation: The ASN platform is built upon the robust, open-source MediaWiki software, the same engine that powers Wikipedia. This provides a world-class, scalable, and secure foundation for our knowledge base.
  • Secure Communications: We utilize end-to-end encrypted services to ensure the privacy and security of our correspondence with partners, volunteers, and clients, respecting the sensitive nature of our work at all times.
  • Hosting Infrastructure: Our initial development and staging environments are made possible through cost-effective and reliable hosting solutions, such as those provided by partners like ifastnet.com. This resourcefulness allows us to direct maximum funding toward our core mission of service and data verification.
